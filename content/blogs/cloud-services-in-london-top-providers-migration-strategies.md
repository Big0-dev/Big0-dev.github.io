## Key Cloud Platforms (AWS, Azure, GCP)

The cloud market is dominated by three major players. Understanding their strengths is the first step in choosing the right foundation for your business. Each platform offers a unique ecosystem of services tailored to different needs, from simple storage to complex AI and machine learning.

### Amazon Web Services (AWS)

As the market pioneer, AWS boasts the most extensive and mature portfolio of services. It's a powerful choice for businesses seeking unparalleled flexibility and a vast global infrastructure. Many companies appreciate its deep feature set and proven reliability for mission-critical applications. Its ecosystem is vast, meaning you can find a tool for almost any task. However, this complexity can sometimes lead to a steeper learning curve.

### Microsoft Azure

For organisations already invested in the Microsoft ecosystem, Azure is a natural fit. It integrates seamlessly with tools like Office 365, Windows Server, and Active Directory. This tight integration simplifies identity management and creates a unified environment for your IT team. Azure is a strong contender for enterprises looking to "lift and shift" existing Windows-based applications to the cloud with minimal friction.

### Google Cloud Platform (GCP)

GCP has carved out a niche with its cutting-edge data analytics and machine learning capabilities. It is an excellent choice for data-intensive businesses and those focused on innovation. Companies often praise GCP for its user-friendly interface and high-speed network. If your goal is to leverage big data or build next-generation AI applications, GCP offers powerful, forward-thinking solutions that can provide a competitive edge.

## Common Migration Strategies

Once you've identified a potential platform, the next challenge is moving your existing applications and data. A successful migration isn't a one-size-fits-all process. It requires a thoughtful strategy tailored to your specific workloads and business goals. Choosing the right approach minimizes risk and ensures you get the most value from your new cloud environment.

### The 6 R's of Migration

Most migration strategies fall into one of these six categories. A thorough assessment of your application portfolio will determine which "R" is the best fit for each component.

*   **Rehost (Lift and Shift):** Move your application to the cloud without making any changes. This is the fastest option but may not fully leverage cloud-native benefits.
*   **Replatform (Lift and Tinker):** Make minor optimizations to your application to take advantage of cloud capabilities, such as moving to a managed database service.
*   **Repurchase (Drop and Shop):** Move to a different product, typically a SaaS solution, to replace your existing application.
*   **Refactor / Re-architect:** Re-imagine your application to be cloud-native. This is the most involved option but offers the greatest long-term benefits in scalability and cost-efficiency.
*   **Retire:** Decommission applications that are no longer needed.
*   **Retain:** Keep applications on-premise for now, perhaps because they are not yet ready for the cloud.

### Phased vs. Big Bang Migration

You can execute your migration in two primary ways. A **Big Bang migration** involves moving everything at once in a single cutover event. While this seems efficient, it carries significant risk and requires extensive planning. A **phased migration** is a more cautious and popular approach. You move applications in small, manageable batches. This allows your team to learn, adapt, and troubleshoot on a smaller scale before tackling more critical systems, significantly reducing potential disruption.
