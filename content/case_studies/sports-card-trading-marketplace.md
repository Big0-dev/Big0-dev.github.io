## The Business Problem

The sports card collecting market has a trust problem. Collectors want to trade valuable cards—sometimes worth thousands of dollars—but existing options are risky. How do you trade 3 Luka Dončić Silver Prizm PSA 10 cards for 3 Zion Williamson Silver PSA 10 cards plus $2,000 cash with a stranger on the internet?

Counterfeits are everywhere. Condition disputes kill deals. And without a trusted intermediary, high-value trades either don't happen or end badly. Collectors were stuck choosing between avoiding the trades they wanted or taking substantial financial risks on informal deals.




## How Big0 Solves the Trust Gap

Big0 addresses the core trust deficit by acting as a verified intermediary for every high-value transaction. Our process begins with rigorous authentication: users ship their cards to Big0’s certified grading experts, who verify authenticity and condition against strict standards. This eliminates the "PSA 10" dispute before it starts. Once verified, cards are stored in our insured, climate-controlled vaults. When a trade is initiated—like swapping Luka Dončić Silver Prizms for Zion Williamsons—the platform locks both sides of the deal. Funds and cards are held in escrow until both parties confirm satisfaction. For cash components, Big0 facilitates secure payment processing, ensuring funds transfer only after card authentication. This end-to-end oversight transforms risky peer-to-peer trades into bank-level secure transactions, empowering collectors to trade confidently at any value level.

## The Security Infrastructure

Trust isn't just a promise—it's engineered into Big0's infrastructure. Every transaction is protected by multi-layer security protocols that institutional investors expect. Our vault facilities feature 24/7 monitoring, biometric access controls, and comprehensive insurance coverage through Lloyd's of London. ## Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is a sports card trading marketplace?
A: A sports card trading marketplace is a secure online platform where collectors can buy, sell, and trade sports cards with other users. Unlike standard auctions, these marketplaces often facilitate direct swaps, like trading a Luka Dončić card for a Zion Williamson card. They provide tools for verifying authenticity and managing complex transactions, solving the trust problem inherent in high-value card exchanges.

Q: How do you trade sports cards safely?
A: To trade sports cards safely, use a dedicated marketplace that offers escrow services and authentication verification. This protects both parties by holding cards and funds until the trade is confirmed. Always verify the condition and grade of cards, such as PSA 10 ratings, before finalizing. A secure platform eliminates the risks of scams and ensures a fair exchange for valuable items like Silver Prizm cards.

Q: What is the best platform for high-value card trading?
A: The best platform for high-value card trading is one that prioritizes security, authentication, and user trust. Look for marketplaces that specialize in graded cards (PSA, BGS) and offer features like trade escrow and dispute resolution. These platforms solve the liquidity problem by connecting serious collectors, making it easier to swap premium assets like Luka Dončić Silver Prizms without the risks of private sales.

Q: Why is there a trust problem in sports card collecting?
A: The sports card market has a trust problem due to the prevalence of counterfeits, condition issues, and scams in private transactions. High-value cards, such as PSA 10 graded rookies, can be worth thousands, making buyers wary. Without a neutral third party, disputes over authenticity or payment are common. Marketplaces solve this by providing verification and secure transaction methods for collectors.

Q: How do marketplaces solve the liquidity problem for collectors?
A: Marketplaces solve the liquidity problem for collectors by creating a centralized hub of active buyers and traders. Instead of searching for a specific trade partner privately, you can list your cards and connect with interested parties instantly. This is crucial for high-value swaps, like trading multiple PSA 10 cards for cash or other players, turning illiquid assets into tradable value more efficiently.
