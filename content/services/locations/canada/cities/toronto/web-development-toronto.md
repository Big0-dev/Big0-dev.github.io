### WCAG 2.0 and AODA Accessibility Compliance

**Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA)**
By January 1, 2021, all Ontario public sector and private sector websites must meet WCAG 2.0 Level AA standards. We build accessibility into every website from the ground up:

**Perceivable Content**:
- Text alternatives for images, icons, and non-text content
- Captions and transcripts for audio and video
- Adaptable layouts that work at 200% zoom
- Color contrast ratios meeting WCAG AA standards (4.5:1 minimum)
- No information conveyed by color alone

**Operable Interfaces**:
- Full keyboard accessibility (no mouse required)
- Sufficient time for users to complete tasks
- No flashing content that could cause seizures
- Clear navigation with skip links and landmarks
- Visible focus indicators for keyboard users

**Understandable Content**:
- Plain language appropriate for grade 8-10 reading level
- Consistent navigation and predictable behavior
- Input assistance and clear error messages
- Bilingual accessibility (French and English)

**Robust Code**:
- Valid, semantic HTML5
- ARIA labels and landmarks for assistive technologies
- Compatible with screen readers (JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver)
- Tested with assistive technologies and keyboard-only navigation

**Provincial Accessibility Standards**
Beyond AODA, we comply with accessibility legislation across all provinces:
- Manitoba's Accessibility for Manitobans Act
- Nova Scotia's Accessibility Act
- British Columbia's Accessible BC Act
- Federal Accessible Toronto Act for federally regulated entities
