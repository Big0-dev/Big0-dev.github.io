### Design and Prototyping Phase

**Bilingual Design Systems**
We create design systems that work seamlessly in both English and French:

- **Flexible Layouts**: Designs that accommodate text expansion (French is typically 20-30% longer)
- **Bilingual Typography**: Font selections that support French accents and special characters
- **Cultural Appropriateness**: Imagery and iconography that resonates with both cultures
- **Component Libraries**: Reusable components with built-in bilingual support

**Accessibility-First Design**
Accessibility isn't an afterthought—it's built into our design process from the start:

- **Color Contrast**: WCAG AA compliance (minimum 4.5:1 for normal text)
- **Keyboard Navigation**: Full functionality without mouse interaction
- **Screen Reader Optimization**: Proper heading hierarchy, ARIA labels, semantic HTML
- **Touch Target Sizes**: Minimum 44x44 pixel targets for mobile interfaces

**Iterative Prototyping**
We create interactive prototypes that stakeholders and users can test:

- **Low-Fidelity Wireframes**: Early validation of structure and flow
- **High-Fidelity Prototypes**: Pixel-perfect designs with interactions
- **Bilingual Testing**: Prototypes in both English and French for complete validation
- **Accessibility Testing**: Screen reader testing, keyboard navigation, color contrast verification

## Canadian Regulatory Compliance

### Accessibility Compliance (AODA and Provincial)

**WCAG 2.0 Level AA Compliance**
All our designs meet or exceed WCAG 2.0 Level AA standards:

- **Perceivable**: Text alternatives, captions, adaptable content, distinguishable colors
- **Operable**: Keyboard accessible, sufficient time, seizure prevention, navigable
- **Understandable**: Readable, predictable, input assistance
- **Robust**: Compatible with assistive technologies

**AODA Compliance for Ontario Businesses**
Ontario organizations must comply with AODA by 2025. We ensure:

- **Public Websites**: WCAG 2.0 Level AA by January 1, 2021
- **Intranet/Extranet**: Full compliance roadmap
- **Mobile Apps**: Accessibility from initial design
- **Documentation**: Accessibility statements and policies

### Privacy and Data Protection (PIPEDA)

**Privacy by Design**
We incorporate privacy principles into design:

- **Minimal Data Collection**: Design interfaces that request only necessary information
- **Transparent Consent**: Clear, understandable privacy notices and consent mechanisms
- **User Control**: Easy access to personal data, deletion requests, privacy preferences
- **Secure Design Patterns**: Visual indicators for secure transactions, privacy indicators

**Bilingual Privacy Communications**
Privacy policies, consent forms, and data handling notices must be clear in both languages. We design interfaces that make privacy information accessible and understandable to all Canadian users.
