## Government Funding & Training Incentives

### Federal Training Programs

**Canada Job Grant**
We help employers access Canada Job Grant funding (available in most provinces/territories) covering up to 50-83% of training costs (maximum $10,000-$15,000 per employee depending on province). We prepare applications, coordinate with provincial delivery agents, and ensure compliance with eligible training criteria.

**Canada-Ontario Job Grant**
Ontario employers can receive up to 50-83% funding (max $15,000 per person, $10,000 employer contribution annually). We navigate COJG application process, provide eligible training programs, and handle reporting requirements.

**Canada-BC Job Training Credit**
BC employees receive refundable tax credit up to $1,400 per year for eligible training. Our courses qualify as eligible training expenses, and we provide documentation for credit claims.

**Sectoral Workforce Solutions Program**
For employers in priority sectors (digital technology, clean technology, advanced manufacturing), we help access federal funding for workforce training initiatives addressing skills gaps.

### Provincial Training Incentives

**Quebec Training Funding (Loi favorisant le développement et la reconnaissance des compétences)**
Quebec employers must invest 1% of payroll in training or pay equivalent to Revenue Quebec. Our training programs qualify for this requirement, and we provide documentation for compliance. Quebec businesses can also access Services Québec funding for workforce training.

**Alberta Job Grant**
Up to $10,000 per person (2/3 government, 1/3 employer) for Albertans training for jobs with Alberta employers. We manage application, coordinate with Alberta government, and ensure eligible program delivery.

**Saskatchewan Training Programs**
Canada-Saskatchewan Job Grant provides up to $15,000 per person. We help Saskatchewan employers access funding for upskilling existing employees or training new hires.

**Atlantic Canada Opportunities Agency (ACOA) Skills Development**
Maritime provinces can access ACOA funding for technology skills development. We assist Atlantic Canadian businesses with application and program alignment.

### SR&ED Integration

**Training as SR&ED Eligible Expense**
Employee training directly related to SR&ED projects may qualify as SR&ED expenditure. We document training programs to support SR&ED claims, including curriculum alignment with experimental development, learning outcomes related to technological advancement, and employee time allocation to SR&ED activities.

**Innovation Skills Training**
Training in emerging technologies (AI/ML, quantum computing, advanced materials) can strengthen SR&ED claims by demonstrating systematic investigation and technological advancement pursuits.

### What's the difference between virtual and in-person training effectiveness?

Research shows minimal learning outcome difference between well-delivered virtual and in-person training. Our virtual training maintains effectiveness through: interactive virtual labs (cloud-based environments accessible anywhere), breakout rooms for group exercises and discussions, live instructor interaction with screen sharing and whiteboarding, recorded sessions for later review, and digital collaboration tools for peer learning. Virtual training advantages include: accessibility for distributed teams, elimination of travel time and costs, flexible scheduling across time zones, and easy scaling for large cohorts. In-person training excels for: hands-on hardware labs, team building and networking, intensive bootcamps requiring focus away from workplace, and certain hands-on security training (physical penetration testing). We recommend hybrid approach: virtual for theory and cloud labs, in-person for advanced hands-on and team events.

Yes, we deliver training across Canadian time zones (Pacific, Mountain, Central, Eastern, Atlantic, Newfoundland) through flexible scheduling and recording.
