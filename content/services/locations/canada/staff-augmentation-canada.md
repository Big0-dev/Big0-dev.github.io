## Why Canadian Companies Choose Big0 for Staff Augmentation

### Understanding Canadian Employment Standards

**Provincial Labor Law Compliance**
Each Canadian province has unique employment standards governing hours of work, overtime, vacation, statutory holidays, and termination. Our staff augmentation contracts and practices comply with provincial regulations in Ontario (ESA 2000), British Columbia (Employment Standards Act), Quebec (Labour Standards Act), Alberta (Employment Standards Code), and all other provinces and territories.

**Contractor vs Employee Classification**
We navigate the complex distinction between independent contractors and employees under Canadian law, ensuring proper classification to avoid CRA reassessment, payroll tax issues, and employment standards violations. Our legal structures protect both client and worker.

**Workplace Safety & Insurance**
All augmented staff are covered under appropriate provincial workplace safety insurance (WSIB in Ontario, WCB in other provinces). We maintain valid clearance certificates and handle all premium payments, protecting clients from liability.

### Work Permits & Immigration Support

**LMIA Process Navigation**
For roles requiring foreign workers, we manage the Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) process with Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC). We prepare supporting documentation, demonstrate recruitment efforts, justify wage levels, and coordinate with Service Canada for positive LMIAs.

**Intra-Company Transfers**
We facilitate ICT work permits for multinational clients transferring specialized knowledge workers or executives to Canadian operations. Our immigration support includes work permit applications, family accompaniment, and settlement services.

**Global Talent Stream**
For high-demand tech positions (software developers, data scientists, AI specialists), we leverage the Global Talent Stream for expedited LMIA processing (10 business days vs standard 12+ weeks). We ensure roles meet Category A (high-wage positions) or Category B (designated referral partner) requirements.

**Provincial Nominee Programs**
We help tech companies access provincial nominee streams (Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program, BC PNP Tech, Alberta Advantage Immigration Program) to attract and retain international talent with pathway to permanent residence.

**Avoiding Deemed Employment**
We structure engagements to maintain contractor status and avoid deemed employment (common law employee) determination by CRA. Our contracts include proper independence indicators, separate tools/equipment, and lack of subordination.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do Canadian employment standards affect staff augmentation contracts?

Canadian employment standards (federal and provincial) govern working relationships even for contractors and augmented staff. Key considerations include: maximum hours of work (typically 44-48 hours/week before overtime), overtime pay rates (1.5x or 2x), vacation entitlements (2-3 weeks annually), statutory holidays (9-13 per year depending on province), and termination notice or severance. Our contracts structure relationships to respect these standards while maintaining flexibility. For true contractor relationships, we ensure proper independence. For employee-model augmentation, we act as employer of record, handling all compliance while client directs day-to-day work. This protects clients from employment standards violations, wrongful dismissal claims, and CRA reassessment of contractor status.

### What's the process for bringing foreign workers to Canada for IT roles?

Bringing foreign workers requires Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) from ESDC demonstrating no qualified Canadians available. Process includes: 1) Job posting on Job Bank and other platforms for 4 weeks minimum, 2) Documentation of recruitment efforts, 3) LMIA application with supporting documents (job offer, wage justification, business case), 4) ESDC assessment (standard process: 12+ weeks, Global Talent Stream: 10 business days for high-demand tech roles), 5) Positive LMIA allows worker to apply for work permit, 6) Work permit processing (weeks to months depending on country), 7) Worker arrival and onboarding. We manage this entire process, leveraging Global Talent Stream for software developers, data scientists, and other in-demand tech roles. For intra-company transfers (existing employees of multinational companies), LMIA can be bypassed with different work permit category.

### How do you ensure bilingual technical talent is truly proficient in both English and French?

Bilingual technical screening includes multiple validation points: 1) Resume review confirming bilingual education and work experience, 2) Separate English and French technical phone screens assessing fluency and technical terminology in both languages, 3) Written communication assessment (emails, documentation samples) in both languages, 4) Technical assessment conducted in both languages to verify ability to work in either, 5) Client interviews conducted partially in English and French. We differentiate between functional bilingualism (can communicate in both) and professional bilingualism (can produce technical documentation, lead meetings, and think critically in both). For Quebec roles, we prioritize candidates from bilingual cities (Montreal, Ottawa-Gatineau) or with Quebec work experience. Most truly bilingual technical talent comes from Quebec, Ottawa, or New Brunswick backgrounds.

### What's the cost difference between Canadian-based and offshore augmentation?

Canadian-based technical resources typically cost 60-150% more than offshore equivalents, but bring advantages in time zone alignment, cultural fit, communication, and domain knowledge. Realistic comparison: Canadian senior developer at CAD 120/hour vs offshore at CAD 50-65/hour represents 85-140% premium. However, effective offshore development requires Canadian oversight (architect, tech lead, or PM), reducing net savings. Optimal hybrid model: Canadian architect + 1 local senior developer + 4-6 offshore developers typically saves 35-45% vs all-Canadian team while maintaining quality. Nearshore (Latin America, Eastern Europe) offers middle ground at 50-70% of Canadian rates with better time zone overlap. We help clients find optimal cost-quality-risk balance based on project complexity, domain sensitivity, and required speed.

### How quickly can you provide augmented IT staff for urgent needs?

For standard roles with Canadian talent, we typically provide shortlist within 1-2 weeks and onboarded resource within 3-4 weeks. Expedited process for urgent needs: Week 1 - Requirements gathering and candidate identification from our pre-screened talent pool, Week 2 - Client interviews and selection, Week 3 - Contracting and onboarding. For highly specialized roles or bilingual requirements, 4-6 weeks is more realistic. Our flex pool model provides fastest access (1-2 weeks) for pre-vetted specialists. For offshore/nearshore resources, time zones enable faster response but cultural/client onboarding may extend integration time. Work permit requirements add significant time (10 days for Global Talent Stream, 12+ weeks for standard LMIA, plus work permit processing), so foreign worker augmentation requires 3-6 month advance planning.

### Can augmented staff work remotely or must they be on-site in Canadian offices?

We support hybrid, remote, and on-site models based on client preferences and role requirements. COVID-19 permanently shifted Canadian tech culture toward remote-first, making fully remote augmentation now common and effective. Considerations: 1) Remote work across provincial boundaries creates tax and employment standards complexity (we handle), 2) Security-sensitive roles may require on-site or Canadian-based remote (not offshore), 3) Government contracts often mandate Canadian physical location (security clearances), 4) Some clients require hybrid (2-3 days on-site, 2-3 days remote) for team cohesion, 5) Offshore/nearshore resources are fully remote by nature. Our contracts address work location, equipment provision (client-provided vs BYOD), cybersecurity requirements, and productivity monitoring. Most Canadian-based augmentation is now hybrid or remote, reducing office space costs and accessing talent beyond major cities.

### What happens if an augmented resource doesn't work out or underperforms?

We offer replacement guarantee for the first 30-90 days (depending on contract). If client is unsatisfied with augmented resource performance, we: 1) Investigate issue collaboratively (performance feedback, skills gap, expectations mismatch), 2) Attempt remediation through training, mentoring, or scope adjustment, 3) If remediation fails, replace resource with no additional recruiting fees. Post-guarantee period, clients can terminate with notice period (typically 2-4 weeks) specified in contract. We maintain bench of pre-screened candidates for rapid replacement. Our account management includes regular check-ins to identify and address performance issues proactively before they become critical. For employee-model augmentation where we're employer of record, we manage performance improvement plans and terminations in compliance with employment standards, protecting client from wrongful dismissal exposure.
