## GDPR-Compliant Data Analytics

### Privacy-Preserving Analytics Techniques

**Data Minimization**
Collecting and processing only data necessary for specific analytics purposes. We implement techniques reducing personal data usage while maintaining analytical value, including aggregation, sampling, and synthetic data generation.

**Anonymization & Pseudonymization**
Techniques protecting individual privacy in analytics datasets. We implement k-anonymity, differential privacy, and pseudonymization (replacing identifiers with pseudonyms) enabling insights without exposing personal information.

**Purpose Limitation**
Ensuring analytics processing aligns with original collection purposes. We implement technical controls preventing unauthorized analytics use, maintain processing records documenting purposes, and enable purpose-specific data access.

**Data Subject Rights in Analytics**
Enabling GDPR rights within analytics systems: data subject access requests (extracting individual's data from analytics systems), right to erasure (removing data from analytics and reprocessing results), data portability (exporting analytics data), and automated decision-making safeguards.

### ICO Analytics Guidance Compliance

**Transparency & Fair Processing**
Clear privacy notices explaining analytics processing, purposes, legal basis, and individual rights. We ensure London customers understand how their data drives analytics and can exercise choices.

**Lawful Basis for Analytics**
Identifying appropriate lawful basis for analytics processing (legitimate interests with balancing test, consent for marketing analytics, contractual necessity, legal obligations). We document basis and implement controls ensuring compliance.

**Automated Decision-Making Safeguards**
When analytics drive automated decisions affecting London individuals (credit scoring, insurance pricing, recruitment), we implement GDPR Article 22 safeguards including human review, explanation mechanisms, and appeal processes.

**Data Protection Impact Assessments**
Conducting DPIAs for high-risk analytics (large-scale profiling, special category data, systematic monitoring). We identify risks, implement mitigations, and consult ICO when required.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do you ensure data analytics complies with London GDPR?

Our GDPR-compliant analytics approach includes: conducting Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) for analytics projects processing personal data, identifying lawful basis for processing (legitimate interests, consent, contractual necessity), implementing data minimization (collecting only necessary data), using pseudonymization and anonymization where appropriate, enabling data subject rights (access, erasure, portability, objection), maintaining processing records documenting analytics purposes, implementing security measures protecting analytics data, providing transparency through clear privacy notices, and consulting ICO for high-risk processing. We work closely with your Data Protection Officer or appoint one if required, ensuring analytics drives business value while fully respecting London data protection law.

### What's the difference between Power BI and Tableau for London businesses?

Both are excellent BI platforms with different strengths. Power BI advantages: seamless Microsoft 365 integration (important for London businesses using Teams, SharePoint, Excel), lower licensing costs (£8/user/month Pro vs £52/user/month Tableau Creator), natural language queries, strong Excel integration, and rapid development. Tableau advantages: superior visualization aesthetics, better handling of very large datasets, more flexibility in complex calculations, stronger data science integration, and cross-platform support. For most London SMEs, Power BI offers better value and Microsoft ecosystem integration. Large enterprises or visualization-focused organizations may prefer Tableau. We help you choose based on your specific requirements, existing technology, and budget.

### How long does data warehouse implementation take?

Data warehouse timelines vary significantly based on scope: simple warehouse (single source system, basic analytics): 8-12 weeks, mid-complexity warehouse (3-5 source systems, departmental analytics): 12-20 weeks, enterprise warehouse (10+ source systems, company-wide analytics, complex transformations): 6-12 months. Modern cloud data warehouses (Snowflake, BigQuery) deploy faster than traditional on-premises solutions. Key factors affecting timeline include: number and complexity of source systems, data quality (poor quality extends timelines), transformation requirements, analytics complexity, and organizational change management needs. We use agile approaches delivering incremental value—first dashboards available within 4-6 weeks even for complex programs, with functionality expanding iteratively.
