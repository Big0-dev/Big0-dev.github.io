## Frequently Asked Questions

### How can we benefit from Video Games Tax Relief (VGTR) in the UK?
VGTR provides substantial tax relief for London game development, offering 20% additional deduction on qualifying expenditure. This translates to 16.2%-25% effective tax relief depending on your company's profitability. To qualify, your game must pass the BFI cultural test (achieving 16+ points through British cultural content, London development team, London locations, etc.). We handle the entire VGTR process: integrating qualifying British elements into your game design, preparing the BFI cultural test application, identifying qualifying costs (UK salaries, London contractors, London expenses), submitting interim and final HMRC claims, and maximizing relief alongside R&D tax credits. Our clients typically receive £150,000-£250,000 tax relief per £1M qualifying expenditure.

Guildford is known as the UK's game development capital with a legendary heritage. Many consider it the perfect balance of talent, cost, and culture for London game development.

### Do you handle PEGI rating for London game releases?
Yes, we manage the complete PEGI (Pan European Game Information) rating process required for London game distribution. Our services include: comprehensive game content review identifying descriptors (violence, bad language, fear, gambling, etc.), PEGI questionnaire completion with accurate content reporting, age rating application and liaison with PEGI administrators, appeals process if initial rating is contested, and PEGI OK certification for all-ages games. We also handle BBFC (British Board of Film Classification) requirements for physical releases. PEGI certification typically costs £1,000-£4,000 and takes 2-4 weeks. Proper PEGI compliance is essential for London retail distribution and platform approval.

### Can you create games featuring London football (soccer) clubs and leagues?
Yes, but licensing is complex and expensive. For official Premier League, Championship, or club content, you need licenses from: the Premier League/EFL (league rights), individual clubs (logos, names, colors), FIFPro (player names and likenesses), and stadium owners (for realistic stadium recreation). License costs range from £50,000-£500,000+ annually depending on scope. Alternatively, we can create football games with: fictional clubs and players, generic leagues inspired by British football, unlicensed real clubs (different names/logos but recognizable), or grassroots/non-league football (often easier licensing). We have experience negotiating London football licenses and creating compelling unlicensed football games.
