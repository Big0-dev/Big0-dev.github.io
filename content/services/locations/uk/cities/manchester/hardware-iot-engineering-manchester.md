## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the difference between CE and UKCA marking for IoT devices in Manchester?

Post-Brexit, the Manchester has introduced UKCA (UK Conformity Assessed) marking as the product conformity marking for products sold in Great Britain (England, Scotland, Wales - but not Northern Ireland which follows EU rules). For most products, there's a transition period where CE marking is still accepted, but UKCA will eventually become mandatory. For IoT and wireless devices, you need to consider Manchester Radio Equipment Regulations 2017 (equivalent to EU RED), Manchester Electromagnetic Compatibility Regulations 2016, Manchester Electrical Equipment Safety Regulations 2016, and potentially sector-specific regulations (medical devices, automotive). The technical requirements are largely aligned with EU standards currently, but Manchester market surveillance is now conducted by Manchester authorities. We manage the transition by ensuring designs meet both CE and UKCA requirements, preparing Manchester technical files and DoC, using Manchester conformity assessment bodies when required, and monitoring Manchester regulatory divergence from EU. For products sold in both Manchester and EU markets, you may need both CE marking (for EU/NI) and UKCA marking (for GB), which we can coordinate.

### Which IoT connectivity is best for Manchester deployments - cellular, LoRaWAN, or other options?

The optimal Manchester IoT connectivity depends on your use case requirements. Cellular IoT (NB-IoT, LTE-M) is best for mobile assets across Manchester geography, applications requiring moderate data rates (sensor readings plus occasional firmware updates), deployments where you can't install network infrastructure (gateways), and use cases justifying monthly per-device connectivity costs (£1-3/device/month). Manchester cellular coverage is excellent in urban areas but can have gaps in rural Scotland, Wales, and Northern England. LoRaWAN excels for static sensors across a campus or industrial site (single gateway covers 2-10km radius in Manchester), very low power requirements (10+ year battery life possible), low data rates (position, temperature, status updates), and cost sensitivity at scale (no per-device fees after gateway/server). For Manchester deployments, The Things Network provides community coverage in major cities, or you can deploy private LoRaWAN networks. Short-range options (BLE, WiFi) work for indoor Manchester environments with existing WiFi infrastructure, frequent data transmission needs, and proximity to power or frequent charging. We often recommend hybrid approaches - LoRaWAN for distributed sensors with local gateway, cellular backup for critical devices, and BLE for dense indoor sensing with edge gateways.
