## UK Regulatory Compliance & Accessibility Standards

### WCAG 2.1 Level AA Compliance

Comprehensive accessibility compliance ensuring your digital products meet legal requirements and serve all UK users:

**Perceivable Content**: Colour contrast ratios meeting or exceeding WCAG Level AA requirements (4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text), alternative text for all meaningful images, captions and transcripts for video and audio content, and content that doesn't rely solely on colour to convey information.

**Operable Interfaces**: Full keyboard accessibility without mouse requirements, sufficient time for users to read and interact with content, navigation patterns that assist orientation and wayfinding, and seizure-safe designs avoiding rapidly flashing content.

**Understandable Information**: Content written in clear British English appropriate for general UK audiences (typically reading age 9-11 for public services), predictable navigation and interaction patterns, input assistance helping users avoid and correct errors, and consistent interface conventions throughout the experience.

**Robust Technical Implementation**: Valid HTML markup, proper ARIA labels for complex interface components, compatibility with assistive technologies used in UK (JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver, TalkBack), and progressive enhancement ensuring core functionality without JavaScript.

### Public Sector Bodies Accessibility Regulations

Full compliance with the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) (No. 2) Accessibility Regulations 2018 for government and public sector clients including accessibility statements meeting regulatory requirements, WCAG 2.1 Level AA conformance for all new services, proportionate approach for archived content, and regular accessibility testing and monitoring.

### GDS Service Standard & Design Principles

For government digital services, we apply all 14 points of the Service Standard including:

**User-Centred Design**: Understanding users and their needs through research with British citizens, testing with users throughout development including users with accessibility needs, and continuous improvement based on user feedback.

**Accessibility & Inclusion**: Designing for the whole user base including most vulnerable and least digitally skilled British citizens, testing with assistive technologies, and providing accessible alternatives for users unable to use digital services.

**Technology Appropriateness**: Progressive enhancement approach, open standards, and technology choices that don't exclude users based on their devices or browsers commonly used in UK.
