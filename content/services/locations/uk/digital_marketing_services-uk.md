## UK Digital Marketing Compliance

### GDPR Marketing Compliance

**Lawful Basis for Marketing**
Identifying appropriate lawful basis for marketing processing: **Consent** (explicit opt-in for email marketing, cookie consent for tracking), **Legitimate Interests** (existing customer marketing, B2B marketing with balancing test), documenting basis and implementing controls ensuring compliance.

**Cookie Consent & Tracking**
GDPR and PECR-compliant cookie consent: no pre-ticked boxes, clear information about cookies, separate consent for different purposes (analytics, advertising, social media), consent before placing non-essential cookies, and cookie consent management platforms (OneTrust, Cookiebot, TrustArc).

**Email Marketing (PECR) Compliance**
Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations requirements: **B2C:** Explicit opt-in consent required (soft opt-in exception for existing customers). **B2B:** Legitimate interests allowed for similar businesses, but respecting opt-outs. **Requirements:** Clear identity (who's sending), honest subject lines, easy unsubscribe (every email), unsubscribe processed promptly (within 28 days).

**Data Protection & Privacy Policies**
Clear privacy policies explaining marketing data processing: what data collected, purposes, lawful basis, retention periods, third parties (ad platforms, analytics), individual rights (access, erasure, objection, portability), and contact details including Data Protection Officer if applicable.

### ASA Advertising Standards Compliance

**Truth in Advertising**
All marketing claims must be truthful, substantiated, and not misleading. We ensure: factual accuracy, avoiding exaggeration, substantiating claims (especially health, environmental, financial claims), clear pricing (including VAT, delivery costs), and honest testimonials/reviews.

**Comparative Advertising**
UK allows comparative advertising if fair and honest. Requirements: comparing like-for-like, factually accurate, not misleading, not denigrating competitors, and verifiable claims. We create compliant comparison campaigns avoiding ASA complaints.

**Influencer Marketing Disclosure**
ASA requires clear disclosure of advertising when influencers promote products/services. Requirements: #ad hashtag prominently displayed, disclosed before interaction needed (above "more" on Instagram), clear to average consumer, and cannot rely on platform disclosure alone.

**Children & Vulnerable Audiences**
Strict protections when marketing to children or vulnerable groups. Requirements: not exploiting inexperience, appropriate content, responsible messaging, and additional protections for age-restricted products (alcohol, gambling, financial services).

### Industry-Specific Regulations

**Financial Services (FCA)**
Financial promotions must be clear, fair, not misleading, include risk warnings, and approved by FCA-authorized persons. We ensure financial services marketing complies with FCA rules avoiding regulatory action.

**Healthcare Advertising**
Medicines and medical devices face strict advertising rules. We ensure healthcare marketing complies with MHRA requirements, doesn't make unauthorized health claims, and includes required disclaimers.

**Alcohol Advertising**
Strict rules protecting under-18s from alcohol marketing. Requirements: age-gating, responsible drinking messages, not appealing to under-18s, and following Portman Group code.

### How do you ensure digital marketing complies with UK GDPR and PECR?

Our compliance-first approach protects UK businesses from ICO enforcement. **Cookie Compliance:** Cookie consent before placing non-essential cookies (analytics, advertising, social), clear information about cookie purposes, separate consent for different types, consent before tracking (not after), using platforms like OneTrust/Cookiebot managing consent. **Email Marketing (PECR):** B2C requires explicit opt-in consent (double opt-in recommended), soft opt-in exception for existing customers (marketing similar products), B2B allows legitimate interests for relevant business offers, clear identity/subject lines/unsubscribe in every email, processing unsubscribes within 28 days. **Ad Platform Data Processing:** Data Processing Agreements with Google, Facebook, etc., Privacy Policy explaining ad platform data sharing, cookie consent controlling ad platform pixels, server-side tracking where appropriate (reducing browser tracking). **Analytics:** GA4 with IP anonymization, cookie consent before analytics cookies, data retention limits (14 months recommended), privacy-friendly alternatives (Plausible, Fathom) if desired. **Documentation:** Privacy policies, cookie policies, consent records, DPIAs for high-risk processing, records of processing activities. We build compliance into every campaign, not as afterthought, avoiding ICO fines and building customer trust.

### How does Brexit affect digital marketing in UK?

Brexit created several digital marketing implications for UK businesses. **Data Protection:** GDPR continues in UK as Data Protection Act 2018 (UK-GDPR), EU-UK data adequacy decision allows continued data flows (but could be revoked), businesses marketing to EU must comply with EU GDPR separately. **EU Marketing:** UK businesses targeting EU consumers must comply with EU regulations, separate privacy policies may be needed for EU/UK, cookie consent must meet EU standards when targeting EU. **Ad Platforms:** Facebook, Google operate UK and EU ad accounts separately, targeting both requires managing across platforms, reporting separated by region. **E-commerce Marketing:** Customs declarations for EU shipments affect marketing (delivery times, costs in ad copy), VAT changes (UK businesses collect VAT on EU sales <€150, marketplace facilitation for large platforms), must communicate delivery timescales honestly (5-10 days for EU vs. 1-3 UK). **Northern Ireland Protocol:** Unique status requires careful geographic targeting, some goods follow EU rules, complex for UK businesses marketing in Northern Ireland. **Opportunities:** UK-specific targeting (focus on British market), regulatory divergence may simplify some UK compliance (vs. EU), English-language advantage for international expansion outside EU. **Practical Impact:** Minimal for UK-only businesses, significant for EU expansion requiring separate compliance, Northern Ireland complexity for some sectors. We navigate Brexit marketing complexities ensuring compliant, effective campaigns.
