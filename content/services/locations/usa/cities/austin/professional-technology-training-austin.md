### Federal Government Training Requirements

**FedRAMP Training and Certification**
Federal agencies adopting cloud services require FedRAMP (Federal Risk and Authorization Management Program) knowledge across technology teams. Our FedRAMP training covers authorization framework fundamentals, security assessment procedures, continuous monitoring requirements, differences between FedRAMP Low, Moderate, and High impact levels, and agency authorization processes. Training serves cloud service providers pursuing FedRAMP authorization and federal agencies evaluating and using FedRAMP-authorized services.

**NIST Cybersecurity Framework Training**
The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) Cybersecurity Framework provides voluntary guidance for managing cybersecurity risk widely adopted by federal agencies and critical infrastructure sectors. Our NIST training covers framework core functions (Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover), implementation tiers, framework profiles, and practical application to organizational cybersecurity programs. Advanced training addresses NIST Special Publications including SP 800-53 (security controls), SP 800-171 (protecting CUI in non-federal systems), and SP 800-37 (Risk Management Framework).

**FISMA Compliance Training**
Federal Information Security Management Act (FISMA) mandates require annual security awareness training for all federal employees and contractors with access to federal information systems. Our FISMA-compliant security awareness training covers information security principles, threat landscape, social engineering prevention, password security, physical security, incident reporting, mobile device security, and acceptable use policies meeting federal training requirements. We provide completion certificates and tracking reports supporting agency compliance documentation.

**DoD and Defense Contractor Training**
Defense contractors and DoD components face specialized training requirements including DFARS cybersecurity training for contractors handling Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI), CMMC (Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification) preparation training, ITAR compliance training for defense articles and technical data, and security clearance training for personnel with clearances. Our defense-focused training addresses unique DoD security frameworks and compliance obligations.

### State-Specific Training Requirements

**California Privacy and Security Training**
California's comprehensive privacy legislation including CCPA/CPRA (California Consumer Privacy Rights Act) and California's data breach notification law (Civil Code Section 1798.82) create training obligations for companies handling California resident personal information. Our California privacy training covers consumer rights (access, deletion, opt-out, correction), business obligations (notice, data minimization, security requirements), enforcement and penalties, and operational implementation. Our DFS-compliant training covers regulation requirements, risk-based security programs, incident response, third-party security, and specific technical requirements enabling financial institutions to demonstrate regulatory compliance.

### Virtual Instructor-Led Training (VILT)

**Live Online Classes**
Virtual instructor-led training delivers live instruction through web conferencing platforms (Zoom, Microsoft Teams, WebEx) replicating classroom experience remotely. VILT provides real-time instructor interaction, hands-on lab exercises, Q&A sessions, breakout room activities, and participant networking while eliminating travel costs and time. Virtual delivery enables distributed teams to train together, provides recording access for review, and supports global time zone coverage through multiple session offerings.

**Technology Requirements**
VILT participants need reliable internet connectivity, webcam and microphone for interaction, dual monitors recommended (one for instruction, one for labs), and modern web browser. We provide cloud-based lab environments eliminating local software installation requirements and ensuring consistent lab experiences across participants.

**VILT Advantages**:
- 40-60% cost reduction compared to on-site training (no travel, venues, catering)
- Flexibility accommodating remote workforces
- Recording availability for review and missed sessions
- Easy rescheduling and makeup sessions
- Identical content quality to classroom delivery

**Typical Pricing**:
- Private virtual classes: $5,000-$10,000 per day (serves 15-25 participants)
- Public virtual enrollment: $800-$1,800 per person for 2-5 day programs

### Hybrid Training Programs

**Blended Learning Approaches**
Hybrid programs combine multiple delivery modalities optimizing learning effectiveness and cost efficiency. Common hybrid models include:
- Pre-work e-learning covering foundational concepts followed by instructor-led advanced training
