## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I ensure my analytics comply with CCPA and other state privacy laws?

CCPA compliance for analytics requires implementing technical controls for consumer rights (access, deletion, opt-out of sale), purpose limitation, and data minimization. Key steps include: (1) Data mapping to identify personal information, (2) Consent management for analytics cookies/tracking, (3) Pseudonymization or anonymization for analytics datasets, (4) Access controls limiting analyst access to identifiable data, (5) Retention policies with automated deletion, (6) Privacy-by-design in new analytics initiatives. We implement privacy-preserving analytics techniques including differential privacy, federated learning, and synthetic data generation. Multi-state compliance requires handling varying requirements across California, Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, and Utah laws.

### What's the typical timeline and cost for implementing Snowflake or Databricks?

Snowflake/Databricks implementations typically take 3-6 months depending on scope. Phase 1 (1-2 months): Platform setup, architecture design, initial data ingestion from 5-10 sources ($75,000-150,000). Phase 2 (2-3 months): Advanced integrations, transformation pipelines with dbt, BI tool connections ($100,000-200,000). Phase 3 (ongoing): ML/AI capabilities, data governance, optimization ($50,000-150,000). Total project costs range from $225,000-500,000 for comprehensive implementations. Ongoing platform costs: Snowflake credits typically $3,000-25,000/month, Databricks DBUs $5,000-50,000/month based on workloads. ROI typically achieved within 12-18 months through infrastructure consolidation and analyst productivity gains.

### How do healthcare organizations build HIPAA-compliant analytics platforms?

HIPAA-compliant analytics requires Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with all vendors, encryption at rest and in transit, access controls with audit logging, and PHI de-identification for secondary uses. We implement: (1) HIPAA-eligible cloud platforms (AWS HIPAA, Azure HIPAA, GCP HIPAA with signed BAAs), (2) Encrypted data lakes/warehouses with field-level encryption for sensitive data, (3) Role-based access control (RBAC) tied to workforce roles, (4) Comprehensive audit logging meeting 45 CFR 164.312(b), (5) De-identification using Safe Harbor (remove 18 identifiers) or Expert Determination methods. Integration with EHR systems (Epic, Cerner) using HL7 FHIR APIs. Analytics environments must pass HIPAA security assessments and maintain continuous compliance monitoring.

### What analytics are required for SEC and FINRA compliance in financial services?

Financial services firms face extensive analytics and reporting requirements. SEC requirements include: Form ADV analytics for investment advisers, 13F holdings reports for institutional managers, Form PF for private funds, and new cybersecurity incident reporting. FINRA requirements include: FOCUS reports (financial/operational data), Order Audit Trail System (OATS/CAT) for order tracking, AML transaction monitoring, and best execution analytics. We build real-time surveillance systems detecting market manipulation, insider trading, and wash sales. Implementation includes: (1) Trade surveillance analytics with pattern detection, (2) Risk analytics (VaR, stress testing) meeting regulatory standards, (3) AML name screening and transaction monitoring, (4) Regulatory reporting automation, (5) Data retention meeting SEC/FINRA requirements (typically 6+ years). Integration with Bloomberg, Refinitiv, and other market data providers.

### How can retailers use analytics for omnichannel optimization and sales tax compliance?

Omnichannel retail analytics requires unified customer view across touchpoints: e-commerce, mobile apps, physical stores, marketplaces (Amazon, Walmart), and social commerce. We implement: (1) Customer data platforms (CDPs) unifying online/offline behavior, (2) Inventory visibility across channels with real-time updates, (3) Unified promotion/pricing engines, (4) Attribution modeling for marketing spend allocation, (5) Fulfillment optimization (buy online pickup in store, ship from store). Sales tax compliance analytics are critical post-Wayfair decision requiring economic nexus monitoring: tracking sales thresholds in all states ($100,000 revenue or 200 transactions in most states), automated tax calculation by jurisdiction (10,000+ US tax jurisdictions), and exemption certificate management. Integration with Avalara, TaxJar, or Vertex for automated compliance.

### What's the difference between Power BI, Tableau, and Looker for US enterprises?

Tool selection depends on existing technology stack and use cases. **Power BI** ($10/user/month Pro, $20/user/month Premium Per User): Best for Microsoft-centric organizations with Office 365, Azure, Dynamics 365. Excel-like interface, AI features, rapid development. Limitations: Less robust for very large datasets, governance challenges at scale. **Tableau** ($70/user/month Creator, $42 Viewer): Superior visual analytics and exploration, strong for analyst/data scientist users. Best-in-class visualization capabilities. Higher cost and steeper learning curve. **Looker** ($2,000-3,000/month base, $50/user): Best for technical teams requiring governed metrics through LookML. Strong embedded analytics and API capabilities. Requires SQL knowledge. Modern approach: Many organizations adopt multi-tool strategies using Power BI for departmental analytics, Tableau for advanced users, and Looker for embedded customer-facing analytics.
