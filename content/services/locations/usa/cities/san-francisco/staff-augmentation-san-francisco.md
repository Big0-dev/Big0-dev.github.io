### San Francisco Employment Law Compliance and Classification

**W-2 Employee Augmentation**
W-2 augmentation provides consultants as employees of our company working at your location or remotely. This model offers maximum control over daily work activities, simplified management (we handle payroll, benefits, taxes, compliance), reduced legal risk compared to independent contractors, and suitability for long-term engagements requiring close integration with your teams. W-2 augmentation eliminates independent contractor misclassification risks while providing workforce flexibility without permanent headcount additions.

**1099 Independent Contractor Engagement**
For specialized short-term projects or specific deliverables, 1099 independent contractor arrangements provide experienced consultants who maintain independence, manage their own taxes and benefits, and typically command premium rates reflecting specialized expertise. We ensure proper classification using IRS common-law tests evaluating behavioral control, financial control, and relationship type, protecting you from misclassification penalties averaging $25,000+ per violation plus back taxes and benefits.

**Corp-to-Corp (C2C) Arrangements**
Corp-to-Corp arrangements engage consultants operating through their own corporations or LLCs, providing business-to-business relationships eliminating employment law concerns. C2C consultants assume responsibility for their own taxes, insurance, and benefits while providing specialized expertise often at premium rates. This model works exceptionally well for niche specialists, architects, and senior consultants with established consulting practices.

**Employment Classification Compliance**
We navigate complex employment classification requirements using rigorous analysis of IRS Revenue Ruling 87-41 factors, Department of Labor economic reality tests, and state-specific classification criteria (particularly stringent in California under AB5 legislation). Our classification processes protect clients from costly misclassification litigation, wage and hour violations, unemployment insurance liability, and workers' compensation exposure while ensuring consultants receive appropriate classification under federal and state law.

### Visa Sponsorship and Immigration Support

**H-1B Visa Challenges and Alternatives**
The H-1B visa program's annual cap (85,000 visas including 20,000 advanced degree exemptions) and lottery system (selection rates around 25-45% depending on year) creates significant challenges for companies seeking foreign technology talent. We navigate H-1B complexities including Labor Condition Application (LCA) requirements, prevailing wage determinations, specialty occupation qualifications, and public access file maintenance while pursuing alternative visa categories when H-1B proves unavailable.

**TN Visa for Canadian and Mexican Professionals**
The TN visa category under USMCA provides streamlined access to Canadian and Mexican technology professionals without annual caps or lottery requirements. We leverage TN visas for eligible computer systems analysts and software engineers, managing occupation category compliance, degree requirements, employment letter preparation, and port-of-entry or consular processing strategies delivering faster placement timelines than H-1B alternatives.

**L-1 Intracompany Transfer Visas**
For multinational clients, L-1 visas enable transfer of employees with specialized knowledge (L-1B) or managers and executives (L-1A) from foreign offices to US operations. We coordinate L-1 petitions demonstrating qualifying relationships between foreign and US entities, specialized knowledge requirements, and managerial role qualifications supporting intracompany mobility strategies.

**Green Card Sponsorship Strategies**
Long-term technology staffing strategies often include permanent residence (green card) sponsorship through EB-2 (advanced degree or exceptional ability) or EB-3 (skilled worker) categories. We coordinate PERM labor certification processes, prevailing wage determinations, recruitment requirements, and I-140 immigrant petition filings supporting talent retention objectives while managing multiyear timelines characteristic of employment-based green cards.

W-2 arrangements provide consultants as employees of our staffing company with us handling payroll, benefits, employment taxes, and compliance while you direct daily work activities. However, 1099 carries significant misclassification risk if you exercise too much control over work methods and schedules. Corp-to-Corp engages consultants operating through their own corporations creating business-to-business relationships eliminating employment law concerns entirely. C2C works best for experienced consultants with established practices. We help determine optimal classification based on your specific situation ensuring compliance with IRS and Department of Labor standards.
