## E-commerce Security & Compliance

**PCI DSS Compliance**
Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard compliance essential for accepting credit cards. Level 1 compliance (processing 6M+ transactions annually) requires annual assessment by Qualified Security Assessor (QSA). Most merchants achieve PCI compliance using payment gateways (Stripe, PayPal) enabling SAQ-A (simplest compliance level) by avoiding card data touching your servers.

**FTC Regulations & Consumer Protection**
Federal Trade Commission regulations including CAN-SPAM Act (email marketing consent and unsubscribe), Mail/Internet/Telephone Order Rule (shipping timelines and refunds), and endorsement guidelines (influencer disclosure requirements). Clear refund and return policies meeting consumer expectations.

**CCPA & State Privacy Laws**
California Consumer Privacy Act and California Privacy Rights Act compliance for e-commerce sites serving California customers (essentially all US retailers). Consumer rights implementation: data access requests, deletion requests, opt-out of data sale, do-not-sell links. Expansion to Virginia VCDPA, Colorado CPA, and other state privacy laws.

**ADA Website Accessibility**
Americans with Disabilities Act digital accessibility requirements. WCAG 2.1 Level AA compliance reducing lawsuit risk (e-commerce sites are frequent targets of accessibility lawsuits). Screen reader compatibility, keyboard navigation, color contrast, and alt text for images.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What e-commerce platform is best for US retailers?

Platform selection depends on business size, complexity, and resources. **Shopify Plus** ($2,000/month+) is ideal for fast-growing DTC brands needing rapid deployment, excellent mobile commerce, and extensive app ecosystem—brands like Allbirds, Gymshark, and Fashion Nova use Shopify Plus. **Adobe Commerce/Magento** (typically $20,000-100,000 annually with hosting/development) suits complex catalogs (10,000+ SKUs), sophisticated pricing rules, and B2B operations—large retailers like Coca-Cola, Ford, and Procter & Gamble use Magento. **BigCommerce** ($400-3,000/month) serves mid-market needing B2B features without transaction fees. **WooCommerce** (WordPress-based) for content-commerce hybrid sites. Platform costs are 10-20% of total e-commerce technology spend—consider payment processing (2-3% of revenue), apps/plugins ($500-5,000/month), development, and hosting.

### How do I handle sales tax across multiple US states after Wayfair?

Post-Wayfair (2018 Supreme Court decision), you must collect sales tax in states where you have "economic nexus"—typically $100,000 in sales or 200 transactions annually (varies by state). Process: (1) **Nexus monitoring**: Track sales by state monthly, register for sales tax permit when you exceed thresholds; (2) **Tax calculation**: Implement Avalara AvaTax ($100-500/month), TaxJar ($19-999/month), or platform-native solutions (Shopify Tax, BigCommerce Tax) for real-time calculation across 10,000+ US tax jurisdictions; (3) **Exemption management**: Maintain resale certificates for B2B customers using CertCapture or TaxJar; (4) **Filing**: File returns monthly/quarterly/annually (frequency varies by state and volume)—can be automated or handled by accountant. Marketplace facilitator laws mean Amazon/Walmart collect tax on marketplace sales. Cost: $3,000-10,000 annually for mid-size retailers including software and compliance services.

### What payment methods are essential for US e-commerce checkout?

Essential payment methods for maximum conversion: (1) **Credit/debit cards**: Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover (represent 60% of online transactions); (2) **PayPal**: 70% of US consumers have PayPal accounts, essential for trust; (3) **Digital wallets**: Apple Pay (critical for iOS users, 55% of US smartphones), Google Pay; (4) **Buy-now-pay-later**: Affirm, Klarna, or Afterpay for orders $200+ (increases AOV 30-50%); (5) **Venmo**: Popular with millennials/Gen Z. Payment processing costs: Stripe/PayPal ~2.9% + $0.30 per transaction, BNPL providers 2-6% + $0.30. Displaying multiple payment options increases conversion 10-15%. Consider Shop Pay if on Shopify (accelerated checkout improving conversion). ACH/bank transfers for high-ticket B2B transactions (lower fees ~0.5-1%).

### How can I offer 2-day shipping like Amazon without their infrastructure?

Competing with Amazon requires strategic fulfillment: (1) **Distributed inventory**: Use 3PL providers (ShipBob, Deliverr, Flexport) with warehouses on both coasts (East and West) and ideally Central US—enables 2-day ground shipping to 90%+ of US; (2) **Amazon Multi-Channel Fulfillment**: Store inventory in Amazon's network, fulfill non-Amazon orders through their system ($5-10 per order depending on size); (3) **Regional carriers**: Supplement UPS/FedEx with regional carriers (OnTrac, LaserShip) for faster, cheaper delivery in specific regions; (4) **Ship from store**: If you have retail locations, fulfill online orders from stores creating distributed fulfillment network. Costs: 3PL fulfillment typically $5-12 per order depending on size/weight vs. Amazon MCF $5-10. Transparent delivery promises (show estimated delivery before checkout) more important than blanket "2-day shipping"—customers accept longer delivery for smaller brands if expectations are set correctly.

### What's required for PCI DSS compliance in e-commerce?

PCI DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard) compliance is mandatory for any business accepting credit cards. Compliance level based on transaction volume: **Level 1** (6M+ transactions/year) requires annual assessment by Qualified Security Assessor, **Level 2-4** (fewer transactions) complete Self-Assessment Questionnaires (SAQs). Simplest approach: **Never touch card data**—use payment gateways (Stripe, PayPal, Braintree) with hosted payment pages or tokenization qualifying you for **SAQ-A** (shortest questionnaire, ~15 questions). This is how 90% of online businesses achieve compliance. If you handle card data on your servers (not recommended), you need full PCI compliance: encrypted storage, secure networks, vulnerability scanning, penetration testing, detailed security policies—annual costs $20,000-50,000+. Annual PCI compliance validation required even with SAQ-A. Non-compliance risks: $5,000-100,000 monthly fines from card brands, liability for fraud losses, potential loss of card acceptance.

### How do I optimize checkout for mobile commerce?

Mobile represents 60%+ of US e-commerce traffic but lower conversion than desktop—optimizing mobile checkout is critical. Key strategies: (1) **Guest checkout**: Allow purchase without account creation (accounts reduce mobile conversion 25-40%); (2) **Digital wallets**: Apple Pay, Google Pay, Shop Pay enable one-touch checkout bypassing form entry; (3) **Autofill & autocomplete**: Address autocomplete using Google Places API, credit card scanning, autofill support for saved browser data; (4) **Minimal form fields**: Request only essential information, single-column layouts, large touch targets (44px minimum); (5) **Progress indicators**: Show checkout steps and current position reducing abandonment; (6) **Trust signals**: Security badges, money-back guarantees, customer reviews visible on checkout; (7) **Mobile-first design**: Thumb-friendly button placement, readable text (16px minimum), adequate white space. Test checkout on actual devices (iPhone 13/14/15, Samsung Galaxy, Google Pixel) not just browser emulation. Mobile checkout optimization can improve conversion 20-40%.
