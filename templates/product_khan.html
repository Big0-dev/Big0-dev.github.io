The Problem

You're wearing 5 hats. Something always slips.

Small agencies (5–20 people) run 3–5 client projects simultaneously. The founder is the project manager, the accountant, the sales lead, and half the engineering team. Projects slip. Invoices go out late. Risks surface after they've already become fires.

Existing tools are either too simple (Trello, Notion) or too heavy (Jira, Monday.com). None of them actually watch your projects and tell you what's going wrong.

How It Works

Four steps. You stay in control.

Khan runs a continuous loop across all your projects.

01

Sees

Continuously monitors tasks, risks, timelines, and budgets across all your active projects. Tracks overdue items, engagement gaps, and burn rate anomalies.

02

Analyzes

Spots issues before they escalate. A sprint 40% behind velocity, a risk probability spike, a client not updated in two weeks — Khan catches it.

03

Proposes

Suggests specific actions with clear reasoning. Not "project at risk" — actual proposals: "Move Task X to Sprint 7, reassign to Dev B who has 12 available hours."

04

Acts

Executes approved changes across your projects. Every action requires your explicit approval. Khan handles the busywork. You make the calls.

Human-in-the-Loop. Always.

All actions require your approval before execution. Khan proposes, you decide. No autonomous changes to your projects, ever. This is a fundamental design principle, not a feature toggle.
